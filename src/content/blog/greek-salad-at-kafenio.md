At Kafenio Avondale in Decatur, Georgia, the Greek salad represents the essence of Mediterranean dining. This classic dish combines crisp romaine lettuce with feta cheese, tomato wedges, cucumber, red onion, kalamata olives, and pepperoncini — all brought together with fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and simple preparation.

## A Dish Rooted in Tradition

The Greek salad traces its origins to traditional village cooking in Greece, where farmers prepared meals using available seasonal ingredients. Originally known as *horiatiki* — or "village salad" — the traditional version featured tomatoes, cucumber, olives, feta cheese, olive oil, and herbs. Modern interpretations have evolved to include additional vegetables like romaine lettuce, onions, and peppers.

At Kafenio, both versions are celebrated. The **Greek/American Salad** builds on the classic with romaine and bold toppings, while the **Horiatiki** stays true to the original village-style format — vegetables, feta, olives, and a simple lemon dressing, no lettuce required.
